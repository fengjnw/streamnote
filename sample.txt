Today we are starting a short lesson on photosynthesis, which is one of the most important processes in biology.

When plants receive sunlight, they use energy from the light to make their own food. The green pigment chlorophyll plays a key role because it absorbs light most effectively in the red and blue parts of the spectrum. That energy is then used to combine water and carbon dioxide into glucose.

You can think of photosynthesis as the foundation of most food chains on Earth. Plants store energy in a chemical form, and animals eventually depend on that stored energy either directly or indirectly. Without this process, life on land would look very different.

In class, it is helpful to remember three simple ideas. First, sunlight provides the energy. Second, chlorophyll captures that energy. Third, the plant turns it into sugar and releases oxygen as a byproduct.

Before we move on, try to explain the process in your own words. If you can summarize it clearly in one or two sentences, you probably understand the main idea well enough for today.
